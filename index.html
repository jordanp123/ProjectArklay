SCME

A training tool for intuitive mine power systems · web edition

About

SCME (Simulated Circuit for Mine Electrical) gives people a more intuitive feel for how adding, removing, and changing components in a mine power system affects its behavior — a place to try “what if I put a bigger transformer here?” and watch the numbers move. It is not an engineering or safety tool, and any such use is strictly forbidden.

Method

Models use the standard impedance method with cable resistance temperature-corrected via the inferred-zero method (T∞ = 234.5 °C for copper). Minimum short-circuit values apply a 5% utility voltage sag and a voltage-dependent arcing-fault factor; breaker checks apply both of those plus the breaker’s manufacturer pickup tolerance.

What’s modeled

Symmetrical three-phase and line-to-line bolted faults on tree-shaped (radial or branched) circuits, with motor contributions to maximum short circuit, a steady-state load flow, and motor-starting voltage dip. DC systems, single line-to-ground faults, and looped or multi-source topologies are not modeled.
